Welcome to ModFest 1.16!

ModFest 1.16 is an upcoming modding event that will run from Friday, June 12th thru Sunday, June 21st, 2020. ModFest is a great excuse to try something new, practice your modding abilities, get starting on a new mod you've been thinking about, or any other reason to make a mod.

Entry requirements:

  • Runs on Minecraft 1.16 pre-releases with Fabric Loader. See 'Version' section below for more info.
  • Cannot cause massive compatibility issues with other entries
  • Mods must be created for this event in the allotted time period. This begins on the first second of June 12th UTC, and ends on the very last second of June 21st UTC.
  • Use of one of the themes is encouraged. Not doing so will put you at a disadvantage when it comes to awards
  • All team members must be present in the ModFest Discord Server
  • Using pre-created libraries, assets, or code is fine if they were not made specifically for your entry. The following are some acceptable use cases (but not all):
    • Using a library you or someone else made as long as it wasn't made with the intention of using it for your ModFest entry
    • Using public domain assets or assets you made in the past that were not made specifically for your ModFest entry
    • Using open source code (of yours or someone else) that was not made specifically for your ModFest entry

You can work:

  • By yourself
  • With a team of your choosing
  • With a team randomly assigned to you

Themes:

  • Adventure
  • Fantasy
  • Sci-Fi

Version:

On June 12th when the event begins, start creating your mod with the latest Minecraft 1.16 pre-release. As new pre-releases are released, you may choose to update as they release or hold off on updating until the end. After June 21st, you will have two additional days to ensure your mod is updated to the latest 1.16 pre-release or stable release if it is out by then.

Keep an eye out on the ModFest discord server for announcements regarding any changes here, as the situation could change depending on what Mojang does.

Awards:

ModFest awards are just for fun and ModFest is not a highly competitive event. The ultimate goal of the event is simple: to make mods and have a good time doing it. If you stress about the awards the whole time, you probably won't have as much fun.

Sign up:

Click 'Sign Up!' below to let us know that you're interested in participating. Make sure to also join our discord server!

Sign ups will close for randomized teams on June 11th (the day before the event begins), and they will not close for solo or custom teams until the submission date, June 21st.
